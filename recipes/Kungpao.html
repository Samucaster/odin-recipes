Kung Pao Chicken

Description

Kung Pao chicken, also transcribed Gong Bao or Kung Po, is a spicy, stir-fried Chinese dish made with cubes of chicken, peanuts, vegetables, and chili peppers. The classic dish in Sichuan cuisine originated in the Sichuan Province of south-western China and includes Sichuan peppercorns.

It is easy to make, and you can be as sloppy with the measurements as you want. They reduce to a nice, thick sauce. Enjoy!

Main Ingredients

  • Deboned, deskinned chicken thigh, 300g.
  • Dried chilis
  • Sichuan peppercorns
  • Peanuts or Cashews
  • Leek 1/3 Stalk
  • 1-inch thinly sliced Ginger
  • 2 cloves sliced garlic
  • 1 tsp cornstarch with 1 tsp water

Ingredients for Marinade

  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1 whole egg white
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp shaoxing wine
  • 1/2 tsp dark soy sauce

Ingredients for Sauce

  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp dark Chinese vinegar
  • 1/2 tbsp shaoxing wine
  • 1/2 tsp light soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp dark soy sauce
  • 1/4 tsp chicken buillion powder

Steps

  1. Deseed, cut and rinse the chilis. Slice the garlic and the ginger. Cut the leek. Prepare the slurry and the sauce.
  2. Add some hot, boiled water to the peanuts, let them sit for ten minutes, and peel them.
  3. Tenderize the chicken with the dull side of a knife, then cut into 1/2 inch pieces.
  4. Mix in the cornstarch well together with the egg white. Add in the salt and sugar to the chicken and mix. Add in the cornstarch-egg white mixture to the chicken and mix. Add the liaojiu wine and the dark soy sauce to the chicken and mix. Let that marinate for 20 minutes.
  5. Deep fry the peanuts at ~150C for five minutes, til nice and golden brown.
  6. Keeping your flame on maximum, toss in the chicken at 185C. Break it apart with chopsticks, and let fry untouched for 10 seconds. Then stir it a bit with your spatula for another 20 seconds, and strain the chicken and drain out the oil.
  7. Heat on low, toss in the chilis and the Sichuan peppercorns. Cook for about a minute til the color of the chilis deepen.
  8. Add in the garlic and the ginger, up the heat to medium high. If you were using one of those optional ingredients for color, this is when you'd add it. Stir-fry for about a minute till everything starts to smell real nice.
  9. Add in the sauce around the sides of the wok. Stir and let it quickly reduce for ~30 seconds, it should start to look almost like a thin syrup.
  10. Add in the leeks. Give em a real brief mix.
  11. Add in the chicken. Stir fry for 30 seconds, tossing it if ya can for a more even coating.
  12. Add in the peanuts. Give it all a brief mix.
  13. Add in the cornstarch slurry. Fry for ~10 seconds to let thicken.
  14. Done.
